WPI Events

Internal events, JoyArt, and WPI's booth at PAX East

My team (from left: Nick Frangie, Jade McEvoy, Tate Donnelly, Jeff Chen, and me), was in charge of organizing, hosting, and overseeing four major events: two internal events for the Interactive Media and Game Development program at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, the PAX booth for WPI student games, and one additional art event affiliated with the program.

JoyArt 2023

Event

JoyArt was an event hosted for two years by Becker College students, intended to celebrate the work of college students studying game art. After the school shut down, so did the event. My team chose to bring it back, and to bring it to WPI's Seaport location in Boston to increase accessibility of the event to schools around Massachusetts. I lead the charge on this particular event.

  • Over 200 submissions received from 12 different schools.
  • 8 Sponsors funded the event, including Demiurge, Zapdot, and Snoozy Kazoo.
  • The event featured finalist artwork in 8 categories.
  • Cash prizes were given out to the 8 category winners and 1 overall winner.
  • A panel of student and faculty representatives from participating schools voted to select event finalists.
  • Five artists from the industry served as judges.

Artists Jasmine Duerk and Kateri Bajer from the game Comet Tail Inn present to judge Zach Schläppi from Demiurge.

My Responsibilities
  • Interview industry veterans for advice on how best to feature artwork.
  • Communicate with game artists to acquire judges for the event.
  • Contact game companies and secure sponsors.
  • Manage submissions and filter them according to panel votes to select finalists.
  • Organize portfolio reviews lead by artists from local game studio Demiurge.
  • Lead setup and organization of the in-person event.

Winners of JoyArt 2023, from left to right: Maya Horta, Jericho Dibb, Brooke Glasier, Adam Ying, Kateri Bajer, Kerri Thornton, Tony Tran, and Mackenzie Reid.

PAX East 2023

Event

PAX East is a gaming convention that takes place every year in Boston, Massachusetts. Since 2013, WPI has contributed funds to set up a booth advertising the IMGD programs and showing off student games. My team was in charge of all aspects of organization and execution of this booth.

  • We showcased five student projects: three 2D projects, and two 3D projects, including one art showcase.
  • We attended all four days of the convention, along with the help of fifteen volunteers.
  • We were in charge of budgeting for the event.
My Responsibilities
  • Took part in the design of the booth to best showcase the displayed projects.
  • Attended the booth, onboarded and organized volunteers, and handled distrubution of badges and materials.
  • Aided in the selection of presentable games that would work best with the nature of the event.
  • Designed T-shirts to identify booth helpers and market the program.
  • Assembled informational posters for games to present to players during the event.
  • Wrote detailed direction for the team taking over the following year.

Alphafest and Showfest

Alphafest

An event that happens around halfway through the school year, providing playtesting oppportunities for student projects My responsibilities included:

  • Organizing game submissions, arranging them in the room, and making sure they are prepared to present.
  • Interviewing student presenters in advance of event preparations to make sure they had everything they needed.
  • Setting up the space with the required tables and materials.

The Emcees, Jen Kindl and Yukon Wainczak from Snoozy Kazoo, talking an IMGD senior about their project.

Showfest

The final event of the school year, Showfest features completed student projects and celebrates their success. A livestream is conducted to show off games around the room, and to broadcast interviews between professionals and seniors. My responsibilities included:

  • Communicating with and hosting the emcees for the broadcast.
  • Directing the creation of the marketing materials to encourage submissions and attendance.
  • Designing, organizing, and setting up the space to best showcase student projects.
  • Coordinated with presenting seniors and found industry veterans for their interviews.
